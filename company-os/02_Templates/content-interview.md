# Template: Content Interview → Draft

Two-part workflow for content (LinkedIn posts, emails to the list, blog posts, video scripts). The system starts with YOUR thinking, not Claude's — that's the difference between assisted writing and polished material that could have come from any agency.

## Part A: Extract the human point

---

I want to create a piece about:

[TOPIC — e.g., "why contractors lose jobs in the first 5 minutes," "what I learned building 10 GHL systems," "Angi leads vs. owned pipeline"]

Do not draft it yet.

Interview me with one question at a time.

Your goal is to uncover:

- what I actually believe;
- what I have observed personally with contractor clients;
- where I disagree with common advice in the marketing/automation space;
- a real example (anonymized client story, real numbers only if verified);
- what failed;
- what the reader should do differently.

Ask no more than eight questions.

After the interview, return:

1. My central argument
2. My strongest example
3. My useful disagreement
4. The practical promise to the reader
5. A proposed structure

Wait for my approval before drafting.

---

## Part B: Produce the draft

---

Now write the piece using the approved structure.

Read:
- 02-customer.md
- 03-voice-and-standards.md
- my interview answers
- any approved research brief

Requirements:

- begin with the contractor's real problem, not a general introduction;
- use my experience and viewpoint as the spine;
- keep factual claims tied to sources or to 01-company.md;
- vary paragraph length naturally;
- use lists only where they improve scanning;
- include concrete instructions or examples;
- do not add invented personal stories or client results;
- do not repeat the opening in the conclusion;
- do not use any banned phrase from 03-voice-and-standards.md.

After drafting, run this check:

1. What sounds generic?
2. What claim needs verification?
3. What section lacks an example?
4. What sentence sounds unlike me?
5. What can be removed?

Revise once before showing me the draft.
Save to 03_Outputs/content-[slug]-[YYYY-MM-DD].md. Publishing stays human.
