# Template: Decision-Ready Research Brief

Use for market, niche, or competitor research (e.g., "should we go deeper on HVAC vs. roofing," "what is Podium/Hatch/Angi doing that threatens us"). The output is a prepared decision, not a pile of links.

---

You are preparing research for a business decision at ModernFlow AI.

DECISION:
[What I need to decide]

BACKGROUND:
[Why this matters now]

RESEARCH QUESTIONS:
1. [Question]
2. [Question]
3. [Question]

SOURCES TO PRIORITIZE:
- official company pages and pricing pages;
- contractor communities (trade forums, Facebook groups, r/Contractor, r/HVAC, r/Plumbing);
- verified review platforms (Google reviews of competitors, G2/Capterra for software rivals);
- government/licensing data where relevant;
- credible recent reporting.

PROCESS:

1. Restate the decision in one sentence.
2. Research each question.
3. Separate verified evidence from interpretation.
4. Look for evidence that challenges my current view.
5. Identify information that could not be verified.
6. Recommend a decision only after presenting the evidence.

OUTPUT:

# Research Brief — [Topic]

## Decision
## Five most important findings
## Evidence table
| Finding | Evidence | Source | Confidence | Why it matters |

## Contradictory evidence
## Unknowns
## Options
## Recommendation
## Next cheapest test

Do not pad the report.
Do not use a weak source when a primary source is available.
Save to 03_Outputs/research-[topic]-[YYYY-MM-DD].md.

---

For repeated research (e.g., monthly competitor checks), save past briefs in 03_Outputs and ask Claude to compare what changed rather than starting from zero.
