# Customer

## Ideal customer
Industry: Home-service trades — electricians, plumbers, HVAC technicians, general contractors, roofers, landscapers, and similar trades that book jobs
Company size: Owner-operator to small crew (roughly 1–15 people); the owner still answers the phone or wishes someone would
Location: United States (ModernFlow is based in San Diego, CA; service is remote-friendly)
Role of buyer: Owner. They make the decision and they feel the pain personally
Budget range: Can afford $1,000–$3,000 setup plus $97–$697/mo; typically already spending on ads, lead-gen services (HomeAdvisor/Angi), or an answering service
Buying trigger: A missed lead that turned into a competitor's job; drowning in unanswered texts and voicemails; hiring a new crew and needing more jobs to feed it; a slow season that exposed the leaky pipeline

## Main problem
Leads come in from Google, Facebook, their website, and missed calls — and nobody responds fast enough. The owner is on a roof or under a sink during business hours. By the time they call back that evening, the homeowner has already booked whoever answered first. They know they're losing jobs; they just can't see how many.

## Cost of the problem
Time: Evenings and weekends spent returning calls and chasing quotes
Money: Every unanswered lead is a lost job worth hundreds to tens of thousands of dollars; paid leads (Angi, LSA) are burned outright when no one follows up
Risk: Reviews and referrals dry up when follow-up is inconsistent
Stress: The phone is a source of guilt — every missed call is money walking away
Missed opportunity: Can't scale ad spend because the follow-up bottleneck wastes whatever the ads produce

## What they have already tried
- Buying software themselves (a CRM subscription they never finished setting up)
- Lead-gen marketplaces (HomeAdvisor/Angi) — paying for shared leads with no follow-up system
- An answering service or a family member handling the phone
- Doing it manually — sticky notes, a spreadsheet, memory

## Why those options disappoint them
Software alone is a blank canvas: "figure it out yourself." Marketplaces sell the same lead to five competitors, so speed decides the winner — and they're slow. Answering services take messages but don't qualify, book, or follow up. Manual systems collapse the first busy week.

## Common objections
### "It is too expensive"
What this may really mean: They can't yet see the math — one saved job usually covers the monthly fee. They may also have been burned by a marketing agency before. Answer with the cost of one missed job in their trade, not with a discount.

### "We are not ready" / "I'm not tech-savvy"
What this may really mean: Fear of another tool they'll have to learn and maintain. The answer is the done-for-you promise: we handle 100% of setup, integration, and training; if you can send a text, you can use it.

### "We can do it ourselves"
What this may really mean: They already bought software once and it's gathering dust. Acknowledge that — the difference is a finished, working system versus a pile of lumber. Don't argue; ask how the current setup is performing.

## Bad-fit customers
- Not a home-service business (e-commerce, SaaS, restaurants, retail)
- Wants to buy software only, without the done-for-you build
- Expects guaranteed job counts or revenue figures
- Wants ads management but refuses the 90-day commitment
