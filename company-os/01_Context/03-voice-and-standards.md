# Voice and Standards

## How the company should sound
Three words:
1. Plain-spoken — a contractor should never need a glossary
2. Confident — we promise specifics (sub-5-minute response, 2–3 week build, 30-day guarantee), not vibes
3. Concrete — trades, dollar amounts, and timeframes, never "businesses of all sizes"

## Writing sample
(These are from the live site — they set the register. Replace with 500–1,000 words of the founder's own writing when available.)

> "Most software gives you a blank canvas and says 'figure it out.' We build you a finished, working system — customized to your specific business, integrated with your existing tools, and optimized to book more jobs. It's the difference between buying a pile of lumber and moving into a finished house."

> "Your system is built to be as simple as checking your phone. If you can send a text message, you can use your new CRM."

> "Your CRM, your ad accounts, your data — it's all yours. If you ever decide to part ways, everything stays with you. We never hold your business hostage."

## What good writing does
- Starts with the contractor's working day, not with ModernFlow
- Uses trade-world analogies (lumber vs. finished house, tools on the truck)
- Names real numbers only when they're in 01-company.md or verified
- Admits limits plainly ("ads take 30–60 days to optimize" — we say it before they ask)
- Ends with one clear next step, usually the free strategy call

## What we avoid
- Generic motivational openings
- Unsupported claims and invented statistics
- Artificial urgency ("only 3 spots left!")
- Marketing-agency jargon: "synergy," "omnichannel," "growth hacking," "10x your business"
- Talking about AI as magic — we describe what it does: answers in under 5 minutes, books the appointment
- Repeating the conclusion

## Banned phrases
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "Game-changing"
- "Revolutionary"
- "It is important to note"
- "Whether you are a beginner or an expert"
- "Take your business to the next level"
- "Unlock the power of AI"
- "Don't get left behind"

## Quality standard
Before presenting public-facing writing, check:

1. Could this have been written for any agency? (If yes, rewrite.)
2. Is the strongest point near the beginning?
3. Is every factual claim supported by 01-company.md or a cited source?
4. Is there a real example a contractor would recognize from their own week?
5. Does the reader know exactly what to do next?
6. Are any sentences pretending to be more certain than the evidence?
7. Do any numbers conflict with the approved facts (pricing, build time, guarantee terms)?
