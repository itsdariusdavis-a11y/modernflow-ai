# Scaling Playbook: Service → System → Product

Three levels. Move up only when the current level is repeatable and documented — each jump trades margin control for volume.

---

## Level 1 — Solo service ($3K–$5K/mo)

- 2–3 clients, everything manual in Claude Desktop / Claude Code
- ~20–30 hours/month total
- **Goal at this level is not revenue — it's assets:** per-vertical prompt library, Soul Character roster, and 2–3 case studies with real ROAS numbers from clients' ad accounts
- Exit criteria: same client, same quality, in ≤6 hours/month, twice in a row

## Level 2 — Systemized ($8K–$15K/mo)

- 5–8 clients at $1,500–$3,000
- Saved per-vertical prompt sets (the docs in this folder become templates with fill-in fields)
- Batch generation days: all clients' monthly packs produced in 2 fixed days, not dribbled across the month
- ~$500/mo VA handles client comms, brief chasing, delivery logistics — you keep only angle curation and QC
- GHL (already in the ModernFlow stack) automates onboarding forms, brief reminders, delivery notifications, invoicing
- Exit criteria: 3+ case studies strong enough that clients buy from a sales page without a call

## Level 3 — Productized ($20K+/mo)

- Templated "UGC Packs" per vertical: client uploads product photos + fills the 6-question brief, an automated pipeline (n8n or similar) runs research → generation → delivery via email
- You sell output, not time

**Named risk: quality dilution.** Automated pipelines lose angle nuance — the counter-narrative step is judgment, and judgment is exactly what the automation skips. Mitigations:

- Keep a human curation gate on the angle doc even in the automated flow (15 min/client, not 5 hours)
- Productize only verticals where you have 10+ proven angle patterns banked
- **Stay at Level 2 until case studies justify Level 3 pricing.** Launching a product with no performance proof means competing on price against every other prompt-wrapper.

## The moat (why this doesn't commoditize immediately)

When every brand can generate its own UGC, production capacity stops being the edge. The durable asset is **knowing which angles convert per vertical** — a dataset built only by running creative through real ad accounts and keeping score. Log every batch: angle, hook, spend, hook rate, CTR, CPA. That log is the business; the prompts are replaceable.

What AI actually replaced here is the agency middle layer — the casting coordinator, the account manager, the $75/hour editor with a 3-day revision cycle — not creative judgment. Volume makes curation *more* valuable. Act accordingly: the winners are the ones who know which angles convert and which are noise dressed in 9:16.
