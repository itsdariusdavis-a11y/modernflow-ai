### Agency playbooks / "agency-in-a-box" programs (positioning ceiling)

Courses teaching "start an AI automation agency" sell at $497–$1,997 but deliver
videos, not working systems. This kit undercuts them while delivering the actual
files — that contrast is the core sales angle ("not a course").

## The pricing decision

| Tier                   | Price    | Anchor logic                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ---------------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| The Playbook           | **$97**  | Above commodity PDF packs ($27–$47), below "course" territory. Low-friction entry that qualifies buyers for the ladder.                                                                                             |
| The Automation Toolkit | **$297** | Top of the n8n-pack range + the playbook layer. Priced where niche GHL snapshots start, justified by runbooks + compliance guides. Expected volume leader (~60% of sales; "Most popular" badge).                    |
| Agency-in-a-Box        | **$497** | Matches the _promo_ price of premium contractor snapshots while including strictly more (creative engine, command-center app, license for client work). Leaves room for a later $997 "+ community/updates" edition. |

Deliberate choices:

- **One-time, not subscription.** Passive means no churn management, no content
  treadmill. A subscription tier can be added later once sales prove demand.
- **Three tiers with a decoy.** $97 anchors low-risk entry; $497 makes $297 look
  like the obvious value; classic good-better-best lifts average order value.
- **14-day refund guarantee.** Comps offer 30–60 days; 14 keeps risk low on a
  file-based product (files can't be "returned") while removing purchase anxiety.

## Platform: Lemon Squeezy (merchant of record)

| Platform           | Fee                                   | Merchant of record (handles sales tax/VAT) |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Lemon Squeezy**  | **5% + 50¢**                          | **Yes**                                    |
| Payhip (free plan) | 5%                                    | No                                         |
| Gumroad            | 10% + Stripe processing (~2.9% + 30¢) | No (VAT on EU digital only)                |

Lemon Squeezy wins for a passive product: at the same 5% as Payhip it also files
sales tax/VAT as merchant of record — removing the one recurring administrative
burden a "set and forget" product would otherwise create. Gumroad's marketplace
Discover feed can be added later as a second channel despite the 10% fee, since
its traffic is incremental.

## Revenue math (per 100 visitors to /toolkit, industry-typical 1–3% conversion)

Assumed mix at 2% conversion: 25% Playbook / 60% Toolkit / 15% Agency-in-a-Box
→ blended order value ≈ $277 → **$415–$625 per 1,000 visitors** after Lemon
Squeezy fees. At a modest 300 visitors/month (SEO page + posts + marketplace
listings), that's roughly **$125–$190/mo initially**, scaling with traffic and
with zero marginal fulfillment cost. Every improvement to the agency's own SOPs
also improves the product (re-run the build script).
